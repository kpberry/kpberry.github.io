Projects

Physics Engine (Processing Python)

  • Current implementation is a particle based engine that handles forces and interactions at arbitrary distances with O(n^2) runtime efficiency
  • Forces can be set up easily based on arbitrary rules by following a Force template
  • Includes variable Gravitational, Electrostatic, Spring, Resistive Fluid Forces
  • Includes, as an example, the "Love" Force, which increases in magnitude as the square of the displacement between two particles and is, ostensibly, the strongest force of all
  • Colors and sizes particles by the force fields they emit and can display the magnitudes of force interactions among all particles
  • Capable of high-precision calculations due to a method that reduces the size and increases the frequency of computations
  • Simulations obey conservation of energy, mass, and momentum (when there are no external forces)
  • Previous version focused on collision detection and could use binary search on ordered pixels to efficiently detect collisions among many arbitrarily shaped objects

Computer Algebra System (Python)

  • Evaluates symbolic expressions of arbitrary parameter lengths
  • Based on regular expressions and basic arithmetic operators
  • Handles Vector and Complex values and their operations
  • Evaluates convergent summations, standard and hyperbolic trig functions, and derivatives
  • Can multiply, add, etc. algebraic functions and perform coefficient simplification and combination
  • Graphs functions, equations, and parametric equations and provides solutions at points
  • Dynamic, context sensitive interface written with TKinter
  • Currently working on a much faster version that properly defines fractions and leverages Python's built-in evaluation method and literal code manipulation rather than a user-defined evaluation method

Plaidify (Processing Java)

  • Takes a foreground image and a cropped background plaid and produces a plaid interpolation of the two
  • Uses the grayscale of the original image and a threshold intensity to determine the "plaidness" of a pixel in the final image to ensure the maintenance of edges and clearly defined shapes
  • Just written for fun, but with good results
